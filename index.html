No-Nonsense Principles

Clarity

Plain language, clear goals

Policies must be explained simply with measurable outcomes, timelines, and accountability built in.

Participation

Decisions with the people

Public meetings, open data, and community oversight—not backroom deals or pay-to-play.

Results

Track, publish, adjust

If a policy isn’t working, fix it fast. Publish metrics so everyone can see progress and gaps.

Dispelling Common Myths

Fact: Public comment and participation are rights. Agendas are public, and your voice can shift votes—especially when neighbors show up together.

Fact: Zoning, budgets, housing approvals, policing policies, and transit routes are decided locally. Local wins stack up to national change.

Fact: Organized voters, consistent testimony, and transparent data beat lobby checks when communities persist and track votes.

City Hall Starter Kit

Quick Start (Tonight or This Week)

  • Find your council’s meeting calendar & sign up for agenda emails.
  • Read the agenda; pick one housing/ethics item to follow.
  • Prepare a 60–120 second public comment with one clear ask.
  • Bring 2–3 neighbors; share a one-page fact sheet (below).
  • After the vote, post what happened and tag officials (be respectful, cite sources).

One-Pager Template

Copy/paste this into your doc tool and add local data.

Title: [Item name & meeting date]
The Ask (1–2 sentences): Approve/Amend/Reject [item] because [reason].
Why It Matters: 3 bullets with local stats.
Who Benefits: renters, homeowners, small biz, unhoused neighbors, etc.
Accountability: Publish monthly metrics on [X], with a 90-day review.
Contact: Your group name & email/phone.

Find Agendas

Search: “[Your City] city council agenda”. Bookmark the clerk’s page. Many cities allow email or RSS alerts.

Speak Effectively

  • Lead with the ask in the first 10 seconds.
  • Use one story + one stat.
  • Respect the clock; share a link to full remarks.

Track the Votes

Create a simple spreadsheet of items, sponsors, votes, and quotes. Publish after each meeting.

Policy Planks

Housing as a Human Right

Homes first, bureaucracy second

  • Build & convert faster: streamline approvals for affordable and workforce housing; convert vacant buildings.
  • Protect tenants & small landlords: right-to-counsel, fair mediation, targeted relief over corporate buy-ups.
  • Transparency: publish monthly permits, completions, vacancies, and waitlist metrics.
Get Money Out of Politics

Democracy, not pay-to-play

  • Local clean-elections: small-donor matching and strict contractor/lobbyist limits.
  • Real-time disclosure: publish contributions and independent expenditures in downloadable open data.
  • Conflict walls: recusal rules with public statements and audit trails.
